How has Canada changed

Since Canada was founded in 1867, it has undergone
many changes both physically, and politically. In 1867,
the population was just over 3 million but now, it is
close to 40 million. Canada has also become one of the
most diverse countries in the world. In 1913, almost
half a million people immigrated to Canada. Nowadays,
almost 300 000 people immigrate to Canada each year.
About 1 in 5 people in Canada were born in another
country. The population has also become much more urban,
with over 80% of people living in urban residences.

One major occurence that changed Canada was the
Industrial Revolution. Due to this, Canada experienced
a huge growth in factory work and industrialisation. In
the early 20th century, a huge number of immigrants
arrived in Canada, broadening the ethnic makeup.After
World War II, Canada experienced an economic boom. The
discovery of oil in Alberta made Canada a leading
petroleum producer.Canada was also one of the most
important in industries such as car-building and
chemical processing. Since the confederation, Canada
has gained a few new provinces, such as Saskatchewan
and Nunavut.
